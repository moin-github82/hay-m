## 1. Executive Summary

HAY-M addresses a well-documented and measurable gap in the UK retail savings market: the inability of low-to-middle income consumers to build meaningful savings habits due to high friction, minimum deposit requirements, and lack of behavioural engagement in existing savings products.

By automating micro-savings through transaction round-ups and combining this with accessible investment portfolio tracking, HAY-M delivers a genuine improvement in consumer financial outcomes — particularly for the 11.5 million UK adults identified by the FCA as having low financial resilience (FCA Financial Lives Survey 2022).

This statement sets out the consumer problem HAY-M solves, the evidence base, the specific benefits delivered, and how the Firm will measure and evidence consumer outcomes during and after the sandbox test period.

## 2. The Consumer Problem

### 2.1 The UK Savings Gap

The United Kingdom has a structural savings deficit among working-age adults:

- **11.5 million** UK adults have savings of less than £100 (FCA Financial Lives Survey 2022)
- **9.7 million** adults have no savings at all
- **46%** of UK adults would struggle to pay an unexpected bill of £300 without borrowing (Money and Pensions Service, 2023)
- The personal savings ratio fell to **8.9%** in 2024, down from a pandemic peak of 23% (ONS, 2024)

The primary barrier is not income — it is **friction and habit formation**. Research by the Behavioural Insights Team (2021) found that consumers who automate savings save on average **3.5× more** than those who rely on manual transfers.

### 2.2 Existing Products Fail Lower-Income Consumers

Current savings and investment products have structural barriers that exclude the consumers who most need help:

| Product | Barrier for low-income consumers |
|---------|----------------------------------|
| Traditional savings accounts | Minimum deposit requirements (typically £1,000+); no behavioural nudge |
| Stocks & Shares ISAs | Intimidating; minimum investment thresholds; perceived complexity |
| Robo-advisors (Nutmeg, Wealthify) | Minimum £500–£1,000 to start; monthly fee erodes small balances |
| Round-up apps (Moneybox, Plum) | Limited UK market penetration; no integrated investment tracking; higher fee structures for small users |

**HAY-M's differentiation:** Zero minimum balance, round-ups from as little as 1p per transaction, integrated savings and portfolio visibility in one app, and a fee structure designed for users with small balances.

### 2.3 The Investment Knowledge Gap

- Only **33%** of UK adults feel confident making investment decisions (FCA Financial Lives Survey 2022)
- **58%** of UK adults have never held any investment product
- Young adults aged 18–34 are the fastest-growing cohort of retail investors but also the most likely to make uninformed decisions without adequate tools

HAY-M's portfolio tracking feature provides a low-risk entry point into investment literacy — users can track holdings, understand gain/loss, and build confidence before committing larger sums, without the Firm providing regulated investment advice.

## 3. Consumer Benefits Delivered

### 3.1 Automated Savings Habit Formation

**Benefit:** HAY-M removes the friction of manual saving by automating round-ups from linked payment cards. Users save without needing to think about it — the psychological barrier of "moving money" is eliminated.

**Evidenced outcome:** Users who activate round-up automation in comparable products (Acorns, US; Moneybox, UK) save an average of £200–£400 per year in micro-increments they would otherwise have spent (Acorns Impact Report 2023; Moneybox Annual Review 2023).

**HAY-M target outcome:** A minimum of 70% of active users accumulate a savings balance of £50 or more within 90 days of activation.

### 3.2 Financial Resilience Building

**Benefit:** Small, consistent savings directly address the "unable to cover a £300 emergency" problem identified in FCA data. A user saving £1–£3 per day through round-ups builds a £300+ emergency fund within 3–6 months.

**HAY-M target outcome:** 60% of users with zero prior savings history achieve a balance of £300 or more within 6 months of using HAY-M.

### 3.3 Accessible Investment Literacy

**Benefit:** The portfolio tracking feature allows users to observe real investment performance — gains, losses, volatility — without risking their own capital. This builds financial literacy and reduces the knowledge barrier to future investment participation.

**HAY-M target outcome:** 40% of users who use the portfolio tracking feature for 60+ days report increased confidence in understanding investment concepts (measured via in-app survey at 90 days).

### 3.4 Goal-Based Behavioural Engagement

**Benefit:** Named savings goals (e.g. "Holiday", "Emergency Fund", "First Home") with visual progress tracking leverage well-documented behavioural economics principles (goal-gradient effect, mental accounting) to increase savings persistence.

**Research basis:** Consumers with named, visualised goals save 2.2× more consistently than those with generic savings accounts (Shlomo Benartzi, UCLA; Save More Tomorrow programme research).

**HAY-M target outcome:** Users with at least one active savings goal retain the app for 90+ days at a rate 35% higher than users without goals.

### 3.5 Reduced Reliance on High-Cost Credit

**Benefit:** By building emergency savings buffers, HAY-M reduces the likelihood that users turn to high-cost credit products (payday loans, credit cards at high APR) to cover unexpected expenses.

**Context:** The FCA estimates that 3 million UK adults use high-cost credit regularly (FCA High-Cost Credit Review, 2021). A £300 emergency fund could prevent a payday loan costing £60–£150 in interest.

**HAY-M target outcome:** 30% of users who achieve a £300+ balance report having used their savings to cover an emergency expense rather than borrowing.

## 5. Consumer Duty Alignment

The FCA's Consumer Duty (PS22/9) requires firms to deliver good outcomes across four areas. HAY-M's design directly addresses each:

### 5.1 Products and Services

HAY-M is designed from the ground up for consumers with small disposable incomes. Key design decisions:
- No minimum deposit or balance requirement
- No monthly subscription fee for core savings features (freemium model)
- Round-up amounts are capped and always visible to the user
- Users can pause or cancel round-ups at any time, instantly

### 5.2 Price and Value

- Round-up micro-savings: free to activate
- The economic value to the consumer (savings accumulated, emergency resilience) materially exceeds any fee charged
- Fee structure reviewed against FCA's value assessment framework; no "loyalty penalty" for long-standing users

### 5.3 Consumer Understanding

- In-app plain-language explanations of all features before activation
- Explicit consent collected for round-up amounts, frequency, and linked account access
- Real-time balance and round-up activity visible on every app launch
- Push notifications for all automated transfers (cannot be disabled — transparency requirement)
- No complex financial jargon; all copy reviewed by plain-language specialist

### 5.4 Consumer Support

- In-app chat support (Mon–Fri, 9am–6pm GMT)
- Email support with 24-hour response SLA
- Instant round-up pause/cancel (no waiting period, no penalty)
- Account closure with fund return within 5 business days
- Vulnerable customer policy: extended support hours for users who self-identify or display vulnerability indicators

## 6. Consumer Harm Safeguards

HAY-M has implemented the following safeguards to prevent consumer harm during the sandbox test period:

| Risk | Safeguard |
|------|-----------|
| Overdraft triggered by round-up | Minimum linked account balance check before any round-up transfer; transfers paused if balance falls below £50 |
| User unaware of savings accumulation | Weekly summary notification; balance always shown on app home screen |
| User cannot access savings in emergency | Instant withdrawal to linked debit card with no fee and no lock-up period |
| Loss of funds (operational failure) | All user funds held in FCA-compliant safeguarding account, segregated from company funds |
| Data breach / unauthorised access | AES-256 encryption at rest; TLS 1.3 in transit; JWT authentication; no card details stored |
| Vulnerable customers over-saving | Spending pattern monitoring with automatic pause if round-ups exceed 15% of monthly card spend |

| Vulnerable customer escalations handled within SLA | 100% | Support ticket data |

## 8. Wider Social Impact

Beyond individual consumer benefit, HAY-M contributes to the FCA's strategic objective of **promoting effective competition in the interests of consumers** and the government's **financial inclusion agenda**:

- Provides a regulated, safe alternative to informal savings clubs (savings pools / "pardners") which are common in lower-income communities but carry no regulatory protection
- Reduces dependence on high-cost credit among financially excluded consumers
- Builds a pipeline of informed, experienced retail investors from demographics underrepresented in UK capital markets
- Demonstrates that micro-savings infrastructure can be commercially viable, encouraging further market entrants and competition
