Dear Innovation Hub Team,

**Re: Application for participation in the FCA Regulatory Sandbox — HAY-M Limited**

We write to apply for participation in the FCA Regulatory Sandbox on behalf of HAY-M Limited, a UK-based fintech developing a micro-savings and investment tracking platform for retail consumers.

**Who we are**

HAY-M is a mobile and web application that enables UK consumers to automatically save small amounts through transaction round-ups and to track their investment portfolios in a single, accessible interface. Our platform is purpose-built for consumers with limited disposable income — a segment consistently identified by the FCA as underserved by existing savings and investment products.

**Why we are applying**

We have developed a working MVP and are ready to test with real consumers. However, our round-up mechanism and savings wallet involve payment services and e-money activities that require FCA authorisation. Before scaling, we wish to test our product, consent framework, and safeguarding model under FCA supervision to ensure our approach is fully compliant — particularly with respect to SCA requirements under PSR 2017, e-money threshold obligations under EMR 2011, and Consumer Duty outcomes.

**What we are proposing to test**

We propose a 6-month test period with a maximum of 2,000 UK retail consumers, a £500 wallet cap per user, and a £50 monthly round-up cap per user. All user funds will be held in a dedicated safeguarding account, segregated from company funds.

**Enclosed documents**

1. Regulatory Perimeter Analysis — sets out which FCA-regulated activities our platform performs, the specific regulatory uncertainties we seek to resolve, and our proposed sandbox test parameters
2. Consumer Benefit Statement — sets out the consumer problem we address, the evidence base, quantified target outcomes, Consumer Duty alignment, and our outcome measurement framework

We believe HAY-M meets the FCA's sandbox eligibility criteria:

- **Genuine innovation:** Integrated round-up micro-savings with investment portfolio tracking in a single app, specifically designed and priced for low-income UK consumers
- **Consumer benefit:** Addresses the savings gap affecting 11.5 million UK adults with less than £100 in savings
- **Regulatory need:** Genuine uncertainty around SCA application to variable micro-amount automated sweeps and Consumer Duty evidence requirements for automated savings products
- **Readiness:** Working MVP with real backend infrastructure, defined test parameters, and a clear exit plan toward Small EMI/PI registration

We would welcome the opportunity to discuss our application at an Innovation Pathways meeting at your earliest convenience.
